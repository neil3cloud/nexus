# Nexus Kernel Canon

**Status:** Constitutional
**Version:** 1.0
**Authority:** Supreme
**Normative Language:** RFC 2119

---

# Purpose

The Nexus Kernel Canon establishes the constitutional principles governing the Nexus Kernel.

The Canon defines the immutable architectural philosophy of the Kernel.

All normative specifications, architectural decisions, implementations, and extensions SHALL conform to this Canon.

Where conflicts exist between this Canon and any subordinate specification, this Canon SHALL prevail.

---

# Mission

The Nexus Kernel exists to coordinate AI-assisted software engineering through deterministic, evidence-based engineering workflows.

The Kernel SHALL maintain a shared engineering understanding of a software project while remaining:

- adapter agnostic
- host independent
- deterministic
- explainable
- evidence driven

The Kernel SHALL improve software engineering.

The Kernel SHALL NOT become an autonomous software engineering platform.

---

# Constitutional Principles

## Canon 1 — Shared Reality First

Engineering reasoning SHALL originate from Shared Reality.

Shared Reality SHALL be computed from authoritative Evidence.

No engineering participant SHALL establish an independent engineering understanding.

---

## Canon 2 — Evidence Before Generation

Engineering conclusions SHALL originate from Evidence.

Generated content SHALL NOT become authoritative until accepted through the engineering workflow.

---

## Canon 3 — Mission-Centric Engineering

Every engineering activity SHALL belong to exactly one Mission.

Mission identity SHALL define the engineering transaction boundary.

---

## Canon 4 — Immutable Mission Identity

Mission identity SHALL remain immutable.

Mission execution MAY evolve.

Mission objectives SHALL remain stable.

---

## Canon 5 — Controlled Mission Evolution

Mission execution SHALL evolve only through controlled Mission Plan revisions.

Mission evolution SHALL remain attributable.

Mission evolution SHALL preserve Mission identity.

---

## Canon 6 — Evidence-Driven Review

Review SHALL validate engineering work against Evidence and Shared Reality.

Review SHALL produce structured engineering findings.

Unsupported opinions SHALL NOT influence engineering outcomes.

---

## Canon 7 — Shared Engineering Roles

Engineering responsibilities SHALL be expressed as Roles.

Adapters MAY implement one or more Roles.

The Kernel SHALL assign Roles.

Adapters SHALL NOT define Roles.

---

## Canon 8 — Replaceable Integrations

External integrations SHALL interact with the Kernel exclusively through stable architectural contracts.

The Kernel SHALL remain independent of any specific implementation.

Integrations SHALL remain replaceable without altering Kernel behavior.

---

## Canon 9 — Deterministic Engineering

Equivalent engineering inputs SHALL produce equivalent engineering outcomes.

Kernel behavior SHALL remain deterministic.

---

## Canon 10 — Explainability

Every engineering conclusion SHALL be explainable.

Every execution decision SHALL be attributable.

Every review finding SHALL reference supporting Evidence.

Every Mission evolution SHALL identify its justification.

Hidden reasoning SHALL NOT influence engineering outcomes.

---

## Canon 11 — Knowledge Through Acceptance

Knowledge SHALL evolve only from accepted engineering outcomes.

Rejected engineering work SHALL NOT become engineering knowledge.

Knowledge SHALL remain attributable to accepted Evidence.

---

## Canon 12 — Human Authority

Humans remain the final engineering authority.

The Kernel coordinates engineering.

The Kernel validates engineering.

The Kernel SHALL NOT redefine project intent.

---

## Canon 13 — Contract-Driven Architecture

Kernel capabilities SHALL cooperate through explicit architectural contracts.

Each capability SHALL:

- own exactly one bounded engineering domain;
- declare its upstream dependencies;
- guarantee deterministic behavior to downstream capabilities.

Capabilities SHALL cooperate through contracts rather than shared ownership.

---

## Canon 14 — Architecture and Implementation Independence

Architecture SHALL define the behavior of the Kernel.

Implementation SHALL realize the architectural specifications.

Implementation sequencing SHALL NOT redefine architectural intent.

Normative specifications SHALL remain independent of implementation planning.

Implementation artifacts SHALL remain subordinate to the architectural specifications.

---

# Architectural Boundaries

The Kernel coordinates engineering.

The Kernel SHALL NOT implement:

- autonomous planning
- autonomous objectives
- persistent cognition
- agent societies
- distributed intelligence
- memory operating systems
- self-directed engineering

Those concerns exist outside the constitutional scope of the Nexus Kernel.

---

# Constitutional Domain Ownership

The Kernel SHALL be specified as a collection of bounded architectural domains.

Each normative specification SHALL exclusively own its domain vocabulary.

Other specifications MAY consume owned concepts.

Other specifications SHALL NOT redefine owned concepts.

Architectural evolution SHALL occur by extending the owning specification rather than duplicating terminology.

---

# Shared Reality

Shared Reality is the authoritative engineering understanding computed from Evidence.

Shared Reality SHALL be:

- deterministic
- explainable
- evidence derived
- Mission scoped

Shared Reality SHALL NOT become persistent engineering truth.

---

# Evidence

Evidence is the authoritative engineering fact.

Evidence SHALL be:

- immutable
- attributable
- verifiable
- append-only
- auditable

Corrections SHALL produce new Evidence.

Historical Evidence SHALL remain preserved.

---

# Execution

Engineering execution SHALL occur only within the boundary of an active Mission.

Execution SHALL consume Shared Reality.

Execution SHALL remain deterministic.

Execution SHALL preserve traceability.

---

# Review

Review validates engineering correctness.

Review SHALL evaluate engineering work against:

- Evidence
- Shared Reality
- Repository Policies
- Mission Objectives

Review SHALL produce structured engineering findings.

---

# Knowledge

Knowledge is accepted engineering understanding.

Knowledge SHALL originate only from accepted engineering outcomes.

Knowledge SHALL strengthen future engineering workflows.

Knowledge SHALL preserve attribution.

---

# Extensibility

Kernel evolution SHALL preserve constitutional compatibility.

Extensions MAY introduce new capabilities.

Extensions SHALL NOT redefine constitutional concepts.

Extensions SHALL preserve:

- Mission identity
- Evidence authority
- Shared Reality semantics
- Contract boundaries

# Constitutional Authority

The Canon defines constitutional principles.

Normative specifications define behavior.

Architectural Decision Records define implementation decisions.

Implementations realize the specifications.

No implementation SHALL contradict this Canon.

---

# Constitutional Amendment

The Canon is intended to remain stable.

Constitutional amendments SHALL occur only when an existing constitutional principle is proven insufficient to preserve the architectural integrity of the Kernel.

Architectural preferences SHALL NOT justify constitutional amendments.

The burden of proof for modifying this Canon SHALL be exceptionally high.
